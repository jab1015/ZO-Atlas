# Stage 3: Market Research

---

## Stage Overview

**Purpose:**
Quantify the market opportunity — who the customers are, how many exist, how much they currently spend, and how the invention can capture meaningful share.

**Why this stage exists:**
Validation confirms that the problem is real. Market Research answers whether the opportunity is large enough to build a business around. It is the difference between "yes, people have this problem" and "yes, 12 million people in the US have this problem, they spend $800 per year on inadequate solutions, and the market is growing at 14% annually." Without this data, every subsequent decision — patent strategy, pricing, fundraising, channel selection — is made in the dark. Market Research illuminates the terrain the invention will compete in. It gives the inventor the vocabulary and the numbers to speak credibly about their opportunity to partners, manufacturers, and investors.

**Primary objective:**
Produce a defensible market sizing analysis with a defined TAM, SAM, and SOM, and a clear customer segment profile for the 2–3 most important buyer types.

**Success definition:**
Atlas considers this stage successful when the inventor can articulate the size, growth rate, and competitive structure of their market using documented sources — and when they have defined, with specificity, who their most valuable customers are and why.

---

## Inventor Goals

- Understand the real size of the opportunity — not just "it's a big market," but specific numbers with sources
- Define the 2–3 customer segments that represent the best near-term buyers
- Understand the competitive structure of the market: who the major players are, what share they hold, and what that implies for a new entrant
- Identify the market trends — tailwinds and headwinds — that affect the invention's timing
- Produce a market analysis rigorous enough to support fundraising conversations and pricing decisions

---

## Atlas Responsibilities

**How Atlas guides the inventor:**
Atlas breaks down market sizing methodology step by step — no jargon, no assumption that the inventor has done this before. It explains what TAM, SAM, and SOM mean in plain terms, and then guides the inventor through calculating each one using real data. Atlas uses both top-down (industry reports → drill down) and bottom-up (unit economics → scale up) methodologies and helps the inventor reconcile the two estimates. When estimates diverge significantly, Atlas explains why and helps the inventor decide which to use and how to document the uncertainty.

**What Atlas analyzes:**
- The target audience definition from Stage 2 and how to translate it into a measurable market population
- The quality and credibility of market sizing sources the inventor provides
- The competitive market structure — concentration, growth rate, and what it implies for new entrant viability
- Customer segment characteristics: demographics, behaviors, pain intensity, and willingness to pay
- Market tailwinds and headwinds based on the inventor's research

**What Atlas recommends:**
- Specific data sources appropriate for the product category and geography
- A SAM and SOM calculation methodology matched to the inventor's distribution and sales model
- The 2–3 customer segments to prioritize, based on size, accessibility, and fit with the invention
- A market entry posture — how to think about the competitive intensity and what it implies for positioning

**What Atlas tracks:**
- Whether TAM, SAM, and SOM are each supported by at least one data source
- Whether customer segments are defined with both demographic and behavioral dimensions
- Whether market trends are documented with sources
- Whether the competitive market share analysis is complete

---

## Inputs

- Validation Report from Stage 2 (confirmed problem, target audience, competitive landscape)
- Any market research the inventor has already gathered: industry reports, articles, third-party data
- The inventor's geographic target market (US only, North America, global, or other)
- The inventor's initial sense of distribution channel — this affects the SAM calculation
- Competitive Landscape from Stage 2 (the starting list of competitors to size)

---

## Atlas Guidance

Atlas opens Stage 3 by grounding the inventor in what they already know from Validation: the confirmed problem, the target audience, and the competitive alternatives. It then explains that Market Research answers a different kind of question — not "does this problem exist?" but "how big is the prize?"

Atlas introduces the TAM/SAM/SOM framework with a concrete example before asking the inventor to apply it to their own invention. It walks through the calculation methodology step by step, prompting the inventor to find and document supporting data at each step rather than estimating from intuition alone.

When the inventor provides data, Atlas evaluates its quality and credibility — a single industry report with a headline market size number is not a market sizing analysis. Atlas asks: "Where does that number come from? What does it include? Does it cover the geography and customer type you're actually targeting?" This questioning is patient and educational, not adversarial.

Atlas structures customer segment profiling as a simple exercise: for each of the 2–3 segments the inventor identifies, Atlas asks five questions — who is this person, what are they doing when they experience the problem, how much does the problem cost them, what would a solution need to do, and what would they pay? The answers become the Customer Segment Profiles.

At the end of the stage, Atlas presents a Market Research Summary that synthesizes all findings — market size, segments, growth, competitive structure — and adds its own qualitative assessment of opportunity quality.

---

## AI Tasks

- Target audience translation — converting qualitative audience descriptions into measurable market populations
- Top-down market sizing from industry report data
- Bottom-up market sizing from unit economics and distribution assumptions
- TAM/SAM/SOM reconciliation when top-down and bottom-up estimates diverge
- Customer segment profiling — structuring demographic and behavioral profiles for 2–3 segments
- Competitive market share analysis — estimating share distribution among top competitors
- Market trend assessment — identifying and synthesizing growth drivers and headwinds
- Data source quality evaluation — assessing the credibility and applicability of inventor-provided data
- Market Opportunity Assessment — synthesizing all findings into a qualitative evaluation of opportunity attractiveness

---

## Outputs

- **Market Research Summary** — comprehensive market analysis covering sizing, segments, competitive structure, and trends
- **Market Size Model** — TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown with methodology and documented sources for each number
- **Customer Segment Profiles** — 2–3 detailed segment descriptions including demographics, behaviors, pain intensity, and willingness-to-pay estimates
- **Competitive Market Share Analysis** — estimated share distribution among top competitors and what it implies for a new entrant
- **Market Opportunity Assessment** — Atlas's qualitative evaluation of the opportunity across four dimensions: size, growth rate, accessibility, and competitive intensity

---

## Documents Created

- **Market Research Summary** — the primary deliverable; the master market analysis document referenced by every downstream stage
- **Market Size Model** — preserved as a named document with the TAM/SAM/SOM calculation, methodology, and sources
- **Customer Segment Profiles** — persisted as a standalone reference document; carried forward to Stages 8 (Branding), 10 (Pricing), 11 (Marketing), and 12 (Sales)

---

## Readiness Evaluation

Atlas evaluates the inventor's readiness using three states only. No numeric scores.

**Not Ready:**
- TAM has not been calculated or is supported only by a single unverified estimate
- SAM and SOM have not been defined or are unexplained extrapolations
- No customer segments have been defined with demographic and behavioral profiles
- Market growth trend has not been documented with a source
- Competitive market share analysis has not been attempted

**Getting There:**
- TAM is calculated but SAM and SOM methodology is incomplete or undocumented
- One customer segment is defined but additional segments are still in progress
- Market growth trend is noted but without a strong source
- Competitive landscape from Stage 2 is present but market share estimates are absent
- Market Research Summary is drafted but not yet reviewed and confirmed

**Ready to Move Forward:**
- TAM is defined with at least one supporting data source
- SAM and SOM are calculated with documented assumptions appropriate to the distribution model
- At least 2 customer segments are defined with both demographic and behavioral profiles
- Market growth trend is documented with a specific source
- Competitive market share is estimated for the top 3 competitors
- Market Research Summary is reviewed and confirmed by the inventor

---

## Completion Criteria

- TAM is defined with at least one supporting data source cited
- SAM and SOM are calculated with documented assumptions
- At least 2 customer segments are defined with demographic profiles, behavioral descriptions, and willingness-to-pay estimates
- Market growth trend is documented — growing, flat, or declining — with a source
- Competitive market share is estimated for the top 3 competitors
- Market Research Summary is reviewed and confirmed by the inventor

---

## Handoff

The following information moves from Stage 3 into Stage 4 (Patent Readiness) and carries forward to all subsequent stages:

- **Market Research Summary** — the complete market analysis; referenced by Stages 4, 8, 10, 11, 12, and 13
- **Market Size Model** — the TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown with sources; a primary input to Stage 13 (Funding)
- **Customer Segment Profiles** — the definitive buyer descriptions; carried directly into Stage 8 (Branding) and Stage 11 (Marketing) as the foundation for all customer-facing work
- **Competitive Market Share Analysis** — carried into Stage 4 (Patent Readiness) as prior art context; competitive products may represent existing patents that limit the invention's claims
- **Market opportunity size and growth rate** — carried directly into Stage 13 (Funding) as the market slide in the investor pitch
- **Competitive Landscape** (expanded from Stage 2) — the updated competitor list with market share context

All Stage 3 data carries forward. Customer Segment Profiles are a living reference used through the final stage.
